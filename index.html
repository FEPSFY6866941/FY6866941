Abstract

The global markets suffered irrefutable change through the recession, triggered by a financial crisis that began in the U.S. housing market and reverberated worldwide. This presentation aims to provide a brief,
yet informative insight into the recession, understanding the 2008 recession and what led to it, alongside its global impact on economic structures, allowing us to comment on the arising financial policies prior/during 
the recession. crises. Furthermore, by studying the recession's causes, government responses and paths towards recovery, we can explore the significant reforms in. Findings reveal that economies experienced a slow but 
resilient recovery, marked by increased financial safeguards and a stronger emphasis on sustainable growth. Concluding with lessons learned, this presentation aims to escalate the importance of ongoing vigilance, 
flexible economic policies, and changes within financial hierarchy, to ensure this doesn’t occur again.
